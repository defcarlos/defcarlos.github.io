The harassment of ideology

An attempt to use non-philosophy

I don’t want to pretend that I understand non-philosophy. However, there is something that’s mentioned within that context that feels understandable to me - ideologies harass us. These harassing ideologies form the logic of how we see the world. Often ideologies are baked into us from an early age. We are told this is how the world works, this is how a person is to think, act, and feel. The result is that we internalize the ideology.

Take the belief that the government is by the people and for the people. The government doesn’t let us be. It requires us to be citizens, tax payers, lawful, patriotic, insert any idea handed down to us from our governments. Some might feel like they enact these roles freely, but (I think) non-philosophy would argue we are being forced to enact these roles. Moreover, the government has a preconceived citizen and forces us to take on that shape. The uniqueness of the individual isn’t respected. We have to force ourselves to fit the role of the citizen that is demanded by the government. To do otherwise opens us up to punishment ranging from being made a pariah, paying fines, to incarceration and even death. I outline this example because I want to show how consequential systems of thought are. They aren’t simply floating around in our heads, they have an impact on the world.

How about a more local example of how ideologies impact our lives - our identity. Let’s take the question, what does it mean to be a man? The question itself presupposes an answer exists. Like a call and response in a ritual. Certainly we can reply by saying, someone with an xy chromosome is a man. Typically, this question is asking us what a man is in our social context and how the role is acted out. Your answer informs your reaction to all sorts of stimuli. Your answer might be something like a man independent, a provider, tough, commands respect, etc. This is one of those harassing systems of thought. The reality is that the question presupposed a certain system of thought with its own internal logic. It forces us to be actors, instead of ourselves.

The unfortunate reality is that we cannot exit ideology completely. We simply leave one ideology for another ideology. The hope is that we can see how much we are harassed by the current ideology we find ourselves in and we exit it for an ideology that’s freer. What might that sort of ideology look like? Instead of asking itself what shape the human can be forced into, it can ask what is the human and what shapes does the human want to be in. If that ideology doesn’t exist we have to create it with our co-conspirators in the pursuit of a freer world.
