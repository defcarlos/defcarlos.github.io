Resisting Bullshit

Anarchist calisthenics aren’t just physical

It’s important for us to maintain a healthy skepticism when ingesting the explanations we are being given by those in charge. Half truths, misinformation, lies, and bullshit are constantly shaping our view of the world. At the macro-level, we can see this in the form of the perception management used by the US government (see this Intercept article on the topic).

On a smaller level let's look at an example of something that might set off your bullshit meter - return to office. Since the lifting of lockdown restrictions a consistent flow of corporations have rolled back hybrid or fully remote work schedules and are demanding workers return to the office full-time. These include things like, improved company culture, informal conversations that can lead to innovations, dynamic problem solving, mentorship, and networking (see this HRD Connect article). The reality is these are only excuses for forcing this change without seeming draconian to the outside world. To anyone that finds themselves working in an office, the reasons are plainly false. Teams had increasingly become distributed, especially in large multinational corporations. You may find yourself never having in-person interactions with people you work with regularly. For anyone with even the slightest sense of incredulity it is clear workers are being recalled because those in charge have felt a slip in their control over their employees. Return to office is a move towards reestablishing control by forcing workers to be in a particular location for a particular amount of time. While in the privacy of their homes workers were able to do all sorts of productive things when there was not much work for them to do - cook, clean, read, rest, spend time with family and friends. In the office these workers are now forced to pretend to be busy or even seek out more work because everyone else seems to be busy and they don’t want the negative attention that comes with being seen as not busy.

What is to be done? Unfortunately, I can only come up with what feels like a facile plan of resistance. We must work towards fine tuning our independent judgement. What I am asking all of us to do is engage in an intellectual form of anarchist calisthenics. Like physical calisthenics, you should start small. Ask yourself does this line of argument make sense? What am I being asked to believe? Apply some disbelief and question. Our ability to apply independent judgement, critical thinking, and logic have atrophied from lack of use. This is no fault of our own. The conditioning required to blindley take orders and follow arbitrary rules is from a lifetime of conditioning. From childhood into adulthood our environment has foreclosed certain options by having rules and laws against them, and by applying social pressure to conform. Like physical anarchist calisthenics, the intellectual variety will prepare us for the day when we are told to believe something truly horrible.
