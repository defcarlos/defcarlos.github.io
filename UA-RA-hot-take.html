Hot take on the Ukraine US-Russia 2025 summit

Hard lessons are being learned about allying with the US

For a long time it has been an open secret (at least state site) that the Ukrainian-Russian war was really a NATO-Russian war occurring in Ukraine using the Ukrainian military. And if we are more honest, NATO is a proxy for the US.

Since taking office Trump has been extremely eager to end the Ukraine-Russia conflict. To the horror of Ukraine and other European Allies Trump has gone forward with initializing talks without them. Let’s not even get into the concessions that Trump has made from the very beginning of these talks. Isn’t it strange that talks are happening with the US and Russia about a conflict between Ukraine and Russia? Talks that when finalized, Ukraine will be forced to accept. It’s all being talked about so matter of factly, it’s almost easy to miss the absurdity of the whole thing. The talks reveal that this has always been a US-Russian conflict with the unfortunate people of Ukraine paying the hefty price.

Another thing on display is an increasingly common pattern. If you are allied with the US, expect to get burned. In the past we have seen this pattern with weaker states, from South Vietnam to the more recent sloppy withdrawal from Afghanistan. We are now seeing it repeat itself in Europe. It’s hard to know what US-European relationships will look like after this. According to the Bureau of Industry and Security’s “The US Trade with the European Union, 2021 (PDF, opens in a new window)”,

In 2021, of the $491.3 billion in U.S. imports from European Union, the top commodity sectors were Chemicals, Plastics, Rubber, and Leather products (31.5%), Machinery and Mechanical Appliances (20.4%), and Transportation Equipment (11.3%).

While bringing the conflict to a quick end early in his Presidency may win Trump some immediate political points at home, it’s hard to imagine this working out in the long run for the working class in our globally connected world.
